                • Alisha Christiansen > Joel D. Galbraith February 21, 2018 at 7:58pm

                  Joel--One interesting thing to consider--that I hope is being weighed by those who are wanting a tutoring option for every online student:

                  I received a forwarded email from a student in South Africa, who said students in her area aren't able to utilize online tutors because it's so expensive for them to be online. They try to do as much work offline as possible. She said:

                  One other problem we encounter is WIFI related.  Data is expensive.  The rate at which purchased data runs out is fast, so it makes the option of online tutoring untenable.  

                  She's a Pathway student, in Math 100, and was explaining why the time in the Gathering was so valuable in helping her classmates to understand the math problems.

                  These Math 100 students each have an assigned online tutor through the campus Math Lab.  It's been a big push this semester from the Course Lead directly to instructors. Instructors have pushed it with their students to use the "Math Help" tutors as an option.  (If the students score less than 75% on a test they are required to meet with a tutor before re-taking the exam.  Re-takes are required for anyone scoring less than 75%.)  From this student's point of view, it quite literally costs the South African students extra money to be able to invest time in working with an online tutor. 

                  I know it's an important part of online course design to keep costs as low as possible for students. Adding in online tutors may be a bit of a hidden cost for some of our international online students. Even if meeting with the tutors is optional, it's an option that may not feel feasible for them.
